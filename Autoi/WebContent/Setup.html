Step Two

Now when the connections are done all you have to do is to provide the iSwitch with your Wi-Fi Credentials. This can be done easily by following these steps:

Stand near the iSwitch and switch it on

Get you phone out and search for Wi-Fi networks. You will see a network with the name iSwitch available for you to connect. The password is “password”.

When connected, open your browser (preferably chrome, but any browser would do). Wait for sign in in this network notification to come then click on the notification.

If the notification does not appear navigate to 192.168.4.1. The following screen should appear.

Click on configure Wi-Fi. Following screen should appear.

Select you Wi-Fi and provide password. Click on save. Image 4.

Wait for 30 seconds. If you have provided correct password, you will see that the phone is now disconnected from the iSwitch Wi-Fi and the Wi-Fi network is now unavailable. You are good to go now.

If the password is incorrect your phone would still be connected to iSwitch after 30 sec. close the browser and reopen and navigate to 192.168.4.1 again. Repeat the following steps.

Download AutoI Android Application from Google Playstore.

Add New iSwitch->Click on Add iSwitch option from the side panel. Enter iSwitch Id and pass key provided during wifi configuration. Enter desired room name and names for devices to be used. Usage

Once an iSwitch has been authenticated and added to the mobile app, it will come up in the side panel with a display name same as the room name provided by user while adding the iSwitch.

Once an iSwitch has been authenticated and added to the mobile app, it will come up in the side panel with a display name same as the room name provided by user while adding the iSwitch.

Click on any of the rooms to enter the room screen. Here an user can do the following:

Switch the devices of that room on/off as per requirement.

Schedule devices to be switched on/off at a later point of time by long pressing on any of the device names.

Edit the details of the room such as room name or device names by clicking on the circular pencil button on bottom right corner of the screen.

Click on Schedules option on the side panel to view the pending schedules. User can also delete any schedule as per requirement by clicking on the red cross sign button.

User can check electricity consumption in graphical format for any of the devices by clicking on the Metrics option in the side panel and selecting the room and the corresponding device from the circular LOV buttons and then clicking on view metrics button.

Password can be changed for an user by clicking on the small pencil icon beside the username in the side panel.

User can navigate to AutoI website by clicking on the side menu button on the right hand side of the toolbar (3 dots) and selecting the AutoI Web option.

A buy now link would also be available on the AutoI website via which user can buy a new AutoI iSwitch.

Electrician Guide

Smile as you have taken the first step. All you have to do now is to tell your electrician to follow the following simple steps to connect the iSwitch to your local Wiring.

1. The first step is to open up the switch board which has the switches which control the devices that you want to automate. Please understand that you can automate up to two devices and both have them should have a physical switch in the same switch board.

2. After the Switch is open and you can see the wires, there are only three connections you have to make.

a. Power to the module
b. Switch 1
c. Switch 2

As you can see there are three connections on the device. The two pin connection on the left most is the power, and the other two are the Switch connections.

3. Connect the power connector in series after any of the switches you want to automate. This enables the iSwitch to be physically switched off using the Switch in case you want to switch off automation.

Now the device has got its power.
All you have to do now is to connect the remaining to connectors to the switches on devices you want to control in series.

In the three pin connector there are three screws, the left most of the three screws has to be left open, it should NOT be connected. You have to connect only the remaining screws, the middle and the right most.

FAQ

What happens when there is a power cut ?

In situation of a power cut, the iSwitch will turn off, and when the power is back on the device will turn on/off as per the physical switch status.

How can I switch the iSwitch off?

As per the connections your iSwitch is connected in series after your physical switch, which means as soon as your physical switch is off, iSwitch powers off, and when it is turned back on it is in “soft off” stat which means to switch iSwitch off you just have to flip the physical switch off and on.

What should the state of the physical switch be while using the APP?

The physical switch should always be turned on for using the Autoi App.

What if my Wi-Fi credentials are changed?

If the wifi credentials are saved and/or your iSwitch cannot find the saved WiFI network, it will host its own Wi-Fi named iSwitch. You can then follow the step one of the setup process to reprogram the Wi-Fi Details.
